üìã What is PRECISE-HBR?

Definition

The PRECISE-HBR (Predicting bleeding complications in patients undergoing stent implantation and subsequent Dual AntiPlatelet Therapy - High Bleeding Risk) score is a validated clinical decision support tool designed to assess bleeding risk in patients undergoing percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) who are at high bleeding risk.

Key Clinical Impact

PRECISE-HBR provides an evidence-based approach to identify high bleeding risk (HBR) patients by:

  • Standardizing HBR Definition: Uses Academic Research Consortium for High Bleeding Risk (ARC-HBR) criteria
  • Predicting 1-Year Bleeding Risk: Estimates BARC 3-5 bleeding events with validated accuracy
  • Guiding Treatment Decisions: Helps clinicians optimize antiplatelet therapy duration
  • Improving Patient Outcomes: Reduces bleeding complications while maintaining ischemic protection
Clinical Impact: Studies show PRECISE-HBR guided therapy can help identify patients who benefit from shorter DAPT duration, reducing bleeding events without increasing ischemic risk.

Target Population

PRECISE-HBR is specifically designed for:

  • Patients undergoing PCI with stent implantation
  • Patients considered for dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT)
  • Patients with potential high bleeding risk factors
  • Clinical decision-making regarding DAPT duration

üî¨ Clinical Evidence

Development Study

Original PRECISE-HBR Study: Derived and validated using data from multiple international registries and randomized controlled trials, including patients from Europe, Asia, and North America.

Study Population:

  • Over 14,000 patients from derivation cohort
  • External validation in multiple independent cohorts
  • Diverse geographic and demographic representation
  • Follow-up period: 1 year post-PCI
Primary endpoint: BARC 3-5 bleeding events at 1 year

Validation Studies

PRECISE-HBR has been extensively validated across multiple populations:

  • C-statistic: 0.68-0.72 across validation cohorts
  • Calibration: Good agreement between predicted and observed bleeding rates
  • Clinical Utility: Demonstrated improvement in risk stratification compared to clinical judgment alone
  • External Validation: Validated in Asian, European, and North American populations
Performance Metrics: PRECISE-HBR demonstrates superior discrimination compared to other bleeding risk scores, with consistent performance across different healthcare systems and patient populations.

Score Development Process

  1. Variable Selection: Based on ARC-HBR criteria and clinical expertise
  2. Statistical Modeling: Cox proportional hazards regression
  3. Score Derivation: Beta coefficients converted to integer scoring system
  4. Validation: Bootstrap internal validation and external validation
  5. Calibration: Assessed using calibration plots and Hosmer-Lemeshow test
